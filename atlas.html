Interactive timeline

Six coordinates in the evolution of heavy sound

A simplified editorial map, not a claim that musical history follows one straight line.

1950s–60s · roots

Amplification changes the room

Electric blues, early rock and roll, louder stages, studio experimentation, and the building blocks of the riff.

1970s · expansion

Heavy, progressive, theatrical

Hard rock, early heavy metal, progressive ambition, arena growth, concept albums, and new guitar languages.

1980s · scale

Neon, precision, and spectacle

MTV-era image, digital production, glam and hair metal, thrash acceleration, synths, and stadium sound.

1990s · fragmentation

Alternative worlds collide

Grunge, industrial, alt-metal, nu metal, black and death metal growth, post-rock, and digital recording shifts.

2000s–10s · networks

Scenes become global feeds

File sharing, streaming, home production, metalcore, djent, revival cycles, and international micro-scenes.

Today · recombination

Genre borders become optional

Rock, metal, electronic, pop, hip-hop, cinematic scoring, and internet-native scenes recombine in real time.
